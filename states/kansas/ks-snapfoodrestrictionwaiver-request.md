As outlined in 7 CFR § 271.1(a), SNAP is designed to promote the general welfare and to
safeguard the health and well-being of the Nation’s population by raising the levels of nutrition
among low-income households.

As outlined in 7 CFR § 271.2 provides the definition of SNAP-eligible foods.

As outlined in 7 CFR § 282.1, legislative authority authorizes to conduct demonstration,
research, and evaluation projects. Additionally, at least 30 days prior to initiation of a
demonstration project, a general notice will be published in the federal register if the project will
likely have a significant impact on the public.

**Summary of Food Restriction Demonstration Project Request:**
_Explain in a few words the goal of the project and its intended outcomes. (250 words)_

Kansas State Senate Bill 125 requires that the Secretary for the Department for Children and
Families (DCF) certify to the members of the State Finance Council that the Secretary has
requested a waiver from the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) to exclude candy
and soda from the definition of eligible foods under 7 C.F.R. § 271.2.

Kansas’s DCF Economic and Employment Services (EES) section requests to exercise Section
17 of the Food and Nutrition Act (2008) to submit a waiver in order to modify allowable
purchases under SNAP. DCF is requesting to exclude all candy and soda from purchase with
SNAP benefits. The proposed waiver will be implemented on a statewide basis 02/15/2027 and
will apply to all SNAP recipients purchasing food within the state of Kansas.

**Proposed Alternative Procedures to Operate Project:**
_Provide a detailed explanation of the proposed Food Restriction demonstration project, and how_
_the State intends to operate the food restriction choice project that differs from how SNAP_
_currently operates._

Kansas’s DCF is recommending excluding all candy and soda from items eligible for purchase
with SNAP EBT benefits. This waiver request addresses public health challenges across KS by
aiming to improve the nutritional well-being of SNAP participants, acknowledging the state's
high rates of obesity and the need to better align with the USDA’s Dietary Guidelines for
Americans (DGAs). The purpose of this waiver is to test and understand how SNAP program
changes impact health outcomes including reducing obesity rates.

_**SNAP-Eligible Food and Food Products Amended by the Food Restriction Demonstration**_
_**Waiver**_

  - _Describe the food(s) of interest that will be restricted under this demonstration waiver._
_What are the desired outcomes of limiting these foods(s)?_

Kansas’s proposed alternative procedure is to amend the exclusions under the definition of
“Eligible Foods.” Under this proposal, candy and soda (also known as soft drinks) would be
ineligible for purchase with SNAP benefits at authorized retailers. The Kansas Retailers’ Sales
Tax Act, KSA 79-3602, defines candy and soft drinks as follows.

"Candy" means a preparation of sugar, honey or other natural or artificial sweeteners in
combination with chocolate, fruits, nuts or other ingredients or flavorings in the form of bars,
drops or pieces. "Candy" does not include any preparation containing flour and shall require no
refrigeration.

"Soft drinks" means nonalcoholic beverages that contain natural or artificial sweeteners. "Soft
drinks" does not include beverages that contain milk or milk products, soy, rice or similar milk
substitutes or beverages that are greater than 50% vegetable or fruit juice by volume.

Kansas is not seeking to change the universal federal restrictions on items such as alcoholic
beverages, tobacco, or hot foods and hot food products intended for immediate consumption.

  - _If applicable, describe incentivization projects to entice SNAP-eligible households to_
_increase purchasing of fruits and/or vegetables._

Kansas is laying the groundwork to increase collaboration with the Double Up Heartland
Collaborative. Part of the national Double Up Food Bucks (DUFB) program, this collaborative is
comprised of a group of organizations working with Kansas and Missouri farmers markets, farm
stands, mobile markets and grocery stores to provide a healthy food incentive program for people
who receive SNAP benefits.

Rev 02/13/2026

This is a powerful initiative that rewards SNAP participants for buying fresh fruits and
vegetables. This intentional collaboration supports the purpose of this waiver request by
providing additional supports to improve the nutritional well-being of SNAP participants. By
making fresh produce more accessible to Kansans who rely on SNAP, we are helping families
put healthier meals on the table, while also boosting local economies by supporting farmers and
food producers across the state.

Many Kansans face serious nutrition-related health challenges, including high rates of obesity,
[diabetes, and other chronic diseases (BRFSS Data Dashboard | KDHE, KS).](https://www.kdhe.ks.gov/2225/Data-Dashboard) At the same time,
rural and tribal communities often struggle with limited access to affordable, healthy food due to
geographic isolation, sparse retail options, and economic barriers.

The DUFB program is a proven, nationwide model that tackles these challenges head-on. It
offers a dollar-for-dollar match on fruit and vegetable purchases, increasing demand at local
retailers and strengthening community partnerships that support local agriculture. This approach
aligns with the goals of the proposed Food Restriction Waiver, to expand access to nutritious
foods for SNAP recipients while restricting the use of benefits on candy and soda.

_**Retailer-level system changes**_
_Describe any retailer-level system changes and/or updates the retailer(s) will incur in the_
_operation of this demonstration project, for example:_

  - _Describe how the retailer systems will be able to support all alternative procedures_
_required of the project, or will it require an upgrade or enhancements?_

Retailers in KS will be required to update their point-of-sale (POS) systems to reflect the
excluded candy and soda under the waiver. These system modifications must support real-time
transaction adjudication, enabling the transmission of Universal Product Code (UPC) or Price
Look-Up (PLU) data at the time of purchase to determine item eligibility in accordance with the
waiver.

Rev 02/13/2026

Kansas’s EBT vendor, Fidelity Information Services (FIS), is not expected to require
modifications to the State’s existing ebtEDGE system, provided that enforcement of item
restrictions remains the responsibility of retailer POS systems. Retailers will be required to
transmit item-level data to the EBT processor for adjudication and submit only eligible items for
authorization. Any attempted purchases of restricted items will be automatically declined at the
point-of-sale.

_changes._

No exemptions will be allowed for retailers.

Modifying SNAP-approved food and beverage options through a food restriction waiver offers a
promising path to enhance public health. By reducing socioeconomic barriers and promoting

  - _Describe how the State will track and evaluate out-of-state transactions in bordering_

While Kansas does not receive item-level data on SNAP purchases made out-of-state, the
State's EBT vendor (FIS) provides monthly reports on all out-of-state transactions completed
with EBT cards. These reports include:

    - Total number of transactions

    - State and zip code of transaction origin

DCF will use these reports to monitor changes in cross-border shopping behavior following
implementation of the waiver. Comparison of online and in-store purchases will be analyzed.
Where patterns indicate a significant shift in purchasing behavior, DCF will include this
information in quarterly evaluations submitted to USDA FNS.
