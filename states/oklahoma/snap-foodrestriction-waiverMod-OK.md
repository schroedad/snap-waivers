Re: SNAP Food Restriction Demonstration Waiver Modification Request

Dear Executive Director Cartmell:

On September 25, 2025, the Oklahoma Department of Human Services (OKDHS) requested to
modify its restricted food definitions of “candy” and “soft drinks” for its Supplemental Nutrition
Assistance Program (SNAP) Food Restriction waiver. Additionally, on November 17, 2025,
OKDHS requested to modify their waiver implementation date from January 1, 2026, to
February 15, 2026.

The Food and Nutrition Service (FNS) approves these modification requests. Oklahoma is
requesting these modifications to its initial waiver request to clarify the State’s restricted food
items and provide necessary additional preparation time for SNAP retailers. FNS agrees that
these modifications will provide a stronger foundation for project success.

This approval is for 2 years, effective February 15, 2026, through February 15, 2028. FNS
provides the modified restricted food definitions and the adjusted project reporting schedule in
the enclosure. All other information, terms and conditions of FNS’s August 6, 2025, waiver
approval remain in effect.

FNS applauds Oklahoma and its SNAP team for the significant efforts made towards the
implementation of the state’s SNAP Food Restriction Waiver thus far. We look forward to
continuing to support your efforts to successfully implement this innovative and impactful
project on February 15, 2026.

**OKLAHOMA SNAP FOOD RESTRICTION WAIVER DEMONSTRATION PROJECT**

**MODIFICATION TO RESTRICTED FOODS DEFINITION**

The Oklahoma Department of Human Services (OKDHS) will restrict “candy” and “soft drinks”
from purchase with Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) benefits, effective
February 15, 2026.

**Definition of "Candy":**
“Candy” means any product marketed or sold as candy, including but not limited to chocolate
bars, gummies, caramels, taffy, licorice, mints, chewing gum, or similar confections. This
includes candy bars or any products with wafers, cookies, or flour components when they are
primarily sold as candy.

Candy also includes non-bakery items that are dipped, coated, or covered in chocolate, yogurt,
or other candy coating, such as chocolate-covered raisins, almonds, or similar products.

This definition also applies to equivalent items, including private-label or store-brand products,
that retailers categorize or market as candy.

Candy does not include:

  - Baked goods such as cakes, cookies, muffins, brownies, pastries, or bread;

  - Items primarily sold as baking ingredients, such as chocolate chips, baking chocolate, or
cocoa powder.

**Definition of "Soft Drinks":**
“Soft drink” means any beverage marketed or sold as a soda, soft drink, sports drink, energy
drink, flavored water, or juice drink containing less than 100% juice. This includes colas, sodas,
teas, punches, lemonades, sports or energy beverages, flavored waters, and fruit drinks that are
not 100% juice.

This definition also applies to equivalent items, including private-label or store-brand products,
that retailers categorize or market as soda, soft drinks, energy drinks, flavored waters, or juice
drinks.

Soft drinks also include all fountain drinks and slushies, regardless of flavor or packaging.

Soft drinks do not include:

- Milk or milk substitutes;

- Coffee beans, grounds, instant coffee, or brewed coffee;

- Plain bottled water;

- 100% fruit or vegetable juice;

- Drinks prepared at home.

**Implementation Approach**
To make sure the new definitions for candy and soft drinks are applied fairly and consistently,
OKDHS will manage implementation at the UPC level, rather than relying on ingredient lists or
individual retailer interpretations. This approach removes ambiguity, provides retailers with a
clear list of restricted items, and ensures SNAP participants see the same rules applied across all
stores. By maintaining and updating a standardized UPC restriction list, OKDHS can give
retailers the tools they need to implement restrictions smoothly while keeping the process
transparent and easy to understand.

Oklahoma’s proposed alternative procedure makes no changes to the provisions that allow
certain populations of SNAP recipients to purchase prepared meals from appropriate facilities
like senior centers, domestic violence centers, substance misuse centers, and homeless shelters
or to universally banned products.
